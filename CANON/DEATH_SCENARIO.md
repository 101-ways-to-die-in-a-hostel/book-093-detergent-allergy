# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Anaphylactic shock triggered by industrial laundry detergent on hostel sheets
**Category:** Circumstantial/Random
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Witnesses:** None initially - roommates sleeping with earplugs

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Severe allergic reaction to sodium lauryl sulfate and optical brightening agents in industrial detergent. Massive histamine release causes throat swelling, airway closure, blood pressure crash, cardiac arrest.

### The Setup
Hostel recently switched to industrial-strength detergent to combat bed bugs (cheaper and more effective). Dorothy has developed contact allergies over the past few years but never had them properly diagnosed - dismissed symptoms as "skin issues."

### The Trigger
Full-body contact with freshly laundered sheets for approximately 15 minutes. Heat from her body releases chemical residue. Absorption through skin and inhalation of off-gassing chemicals triggers cascade reaction.

### The Process
1. Minute 5: Skin begins to itch - she scratches absently, half-asleep
2. Minute 10: Breathing becomes difficult - attributed to "stuffy room"
3. Minute 15: Hives erupting, throat tightening - she realizes something is wrong
4. Minute 18: Tries to call for help, can barely whisper
5. Minute 20: Full anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest, death

## Research Notes
- Contact dermatitis and anaphylaxis from laundry products is documented but rare
- Elderly more susceptible to developing new allergies
- Industrial detergents contain harsher chemicals than household products
- Source: Allergy and immunology journals on chemical sensitization
- Source: Case studies on occupational chemical exposure

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Dot has a wonderful day. Visits temples, haggles successfully at market, feels young. Returns tired but satisfied. The hostel has changed her sheets while she was out - she's pleased. "Finally, a hostel that maintains standards."

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
8:45 PM. She climbs into bed with her book. The sheets feel wonderfully crisp. She comments to her dozing roommate how nice they smell. She falls asleep by 9:15.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)
Write this sequence in full detail:

**Minute 0-10:**
Sleeping peacefully. A frown crosses her face. Hands move to scratch her arms. Legs shift. Her subconscious registers discomfort before her consciousness does. She murmurs Harold's name.

**Minute 11-15:**
Eyes flutter open. Something wrong. Itching everywhere. She sits up, confused. In the dark dorm, she sees hives rising on her arms. Her breathing is wrong - wheezy, labored. She tries to take a deep breath and can't fill her lungs.

**Minute 16-18:**
Panic. She knows what anaphylaxis is - she's a librarian, she's read about it. She fumbles for her phone. Drops it. Tries to call out - her voice is a whisper. Her throat is closing. She sees a roommate with earplugs in, sleeping ten feet away and unreachable.

**Final Moment:**
Dot collapses back onto the sheets - the very thing killing her is the last thing she touches. Her vision tunnels. She thinks of Harold. She thinks of the library. She thinks, absurdly, that she never finished the book she was reading. Her heart, strained beyond capacity, gives up. At 9:37 PM, Dorothy Pemberton dies in crisp, clean sheets.

### Immediate Aftermath
Discovered at 6 AM by roommate waking for bathroom. Body cool, face swollen beyond recognition. Initial assumption: heart attack. Autopsy later reveals anaphylaxis. Hostel quietly switches detergent brands. No charges filed - how could anyone have known?

## Sensory Details

### Sight
The dark of the dorm room. The faint light from a charging phone. Her own arms, covered in hives. The ceiling she can't focus on. The darkness that creeps in from the edges.

### Sound
Her own ragged breathing. The gentle snoring of oblivious roommates. The wheeze that replaces her voice. Then nothing.

### Touch/Pain
The itch - everywhere. The burn of hives. The sheets that felt so crisp now feel like sandpaper on fire. The terrible pressure in her chest. The heat flooding her body, then the cold.

### Smell
The floral chemical scent of the detergent - the last thing she'll ever smell. She dies surrounded by artificial lavender.

### Taste
Metallic, then nothing as her tongue swells.

## The Horror Element
The horror is the mundanity. Everyone has slept in sheets washed with detergent. Everyone has ignored a slight itch. Dorothy dies from an everyday action - going to bed. The thing that was supposed to be clean, safe, comfortable, kills her. And she dies slowly enough to know exactly what's happening.

## The Comedy Element
Dorothy specifically chose this hostel because reviews praised the "clean sheets." She complained about dirty hostels for years. She died in the cleanest sheets she'd encountered on her entire trip. The detergent was used to kill bed bugs - she escaped one death only to meet another.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
Slow build during the day - Dorothy feeling alive, productive, vital. Quick death sequence - we don't dwell excessively but we stay in her head as she realizes what's happening.

### POV During Death
Tight third person in Dorothy's head. Her thoughts become simpler, more fragmented as oxygen deprivation sets in. Final thoughts should be poignant - memories, regrets, Harold.

### Graphic Content Level
- 6/10 - Focus on the internal experience (panic, suffocation sensation) rather than graphic physical details
- Show: the psychological horror of dying alone while surrounded by people. Imply: the physical degradation
